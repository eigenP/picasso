## 2025-05-28 - [Mathematical Equivalence of Mutual Information and Marginal Entropy]
**Learning:** For additive models $Y_{new} = Y - \alpha X$, minimizing the mutual information $I(X; Y_{new})$ is mathematically equivalent to minimizing the differential marginal entropy $H(Y_{new})$. Because $I(X; Y_{new}) = H(Y_{new}) - H(Y_{new} | X)$, and $H(Y_{new} | X) = H(Y - \alpha X | X) = H(Y | X)$ which is a constant invariant to $\alpha$, the conditional entropy drops out of the optimization landscape. This exact equivalence allows reducing a noisy, high-variance 2D histogram estimation into a robust, faster 1D estimation.
**Action:** Always prefer minimizing marginal entropy over mutual information when separating strictly additive components where the conditional distribution shifts deterministically without changing its shape.
