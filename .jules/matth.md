## 2024-04-16 - [1D Optimization for Empirical Mutual Information]
**Learning:** Empirical mutual information, calculated via histogram binning, is inherently piecewise-constant and non-differentiable. Therefore, employing n-dimensional constrained optimization algorithms (like COBYLA) that rely on linear approximations (simplices) is theoretically unsound for a 1D scalar function. COBYLA tends to take erratic steps or get stuck due to the non-smooth topology of the histogram-based objective function. Brent's method (specifically using `minimize_scalar` with `method='bounded'`), however, relies on golden-section search and inverse parabolic interpolation. This combination explicitly manages non-smoothness and robustly brackets the minimum without relying on derivative approximations, making it the canonically appropriate approach for 1D scalar minimization of empirical mutual information.
**Action:** Always prefer robust, derivative-free scalar minimization techniques (like Brent's method) over n-dimensional solvers when optimizing 1D non-smooth or piecewise-constant objective functions.
