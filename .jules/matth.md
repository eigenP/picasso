## 2025-02-20 - Miller-Madow Bias Thresholding in Mutual Information Optimization
**Learning:** Empirical mutual information computed via 2D histograms systematically overestimates the true mutual information, particularly when signals are independent. Without accounting for this bias, continuous optimization algorithms will fit the empirical noise floor, leading to hallucination of unphysical unmixing coefficients (i.e., treating independent signals as slightly correlated). Replacing `fmin_cobyla` with `minimize_scalar` (Brent's method) combined with strict statistical thresholding based on this bias (`bins / (2 * N)`) correctly rejects spurious updates and improves computational efficiency. We also found that `scipy.optimize.minimize_scalar(method='bounded')` ignores the `bracket` parameter, meaning we cannot warm start it. However, because our search space is strictly 1D and bounded between `[0, 1]`, the lack of warm start is not a major issue.
**Action:** When evaluating discrete empirical entropy or mutual information for optimization, explicitly compute the degrees-of-freedom-dependent bias and use it as an early rejection threshold. Never rely on the optimizer's numerical tolerance alone to prevent overfitting to independent distributions.
