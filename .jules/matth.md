## 2024-05-24 - Brent's Method and Miller-Madow Bias for Mutual Information Optimization
**Learning:** Optimizing empirical mutual information using simple gradient-free methods like COBYLA without thresholding is mathematically unsound because discrete histogram estimators have a noise floor. Even purely independent sources yield non-zero mutual information, and optimization algorithms can "fit the noise," hallucinating small degrees of crosstalk to marginally reduce entropy. This is especially true for piecewise-constant estimators like 2D histograms.
The expected finite-sample bias of the plug-in MI estimator is asymptotically the Miller-Madow bias, approximately `bins / (2 * N)` under the null hypothesis of independence. A pure line search optimization using Brent's method is more robust for piecewise topologies than COBYLA.
**Action:** Replace COBYLA with Brent's method (`minimize_scalar(method='bounded')`) for 1D line searches in `minimize_mi`. Apply statistical thresholding to the objective reduction: if the optimized reduction in mutual information ($\Delta I$) is less than the Miller-Madow asymptotic bias (`bins / (2 * N)`), reject the optimization and return an alpha of 0.0, thereby enforcing a principled prior of zero-crosstalk unless evidence is statistically significant.
